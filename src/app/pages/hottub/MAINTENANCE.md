# Hot Tub Maintenance Schedule
### 394-Gallon Bromine Tub

---

## Target Water Chemistry

| Parameter | Target Range |
|---|---|
| Bromine | 3–5 ppm |
| pH | 7.4–7.6 |
| Total Alkalinity (TA) | 80–120 ppm |
| Calcium Hardness | 150–250 ppm |
| Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) | < 1,500 ppm above baseline |

---

## Dosing Reference for 394 Gallons (~1,491 L)

These are approximate adjustments — always test first, then dose.

**Bromine Tablets (1" BCDMH tablets, ~20g each)**
Load 3–5 tablets into your floater to start. At a medium-open dial, expect to replenish **3–4 tablets (~60–80g)** per week. Adjust based on test strips — you want 3–5 ppm.

**Sodium Bromide Granules (initial bromide bank)**
One-time dose when filling fresh water: **56g (2 oz)** to establish the bromide reserve. Follow immediately with a non-chlorine shock to activate.

**Non-Chlorine Shock / MPS (potassium monopersulfate)**
Use to oxidize and activate the bromide bank: **45g (~1.6 oz)** per treatment.

**pH Increaser (sodium carbonate / soda ash)**
Raises pH ~0.2 per **6g** in 394 gallons. Dose slowly, retest after 2 hours.

**pH Decreaser (sodium bisulfate / dry acid)**
Lowers pH ~0.2 per **6g** in 394 gallons. Add near a jet inlet.

**Total Alkalinity Increaser (sodium bicarbonate)**
Raises TA ~10 ppm per **6g** in 394 gallons. Fix TA before pH.

**Calcium Hardness Increaser (calcium chloride)**
Raises CH ~10 ppm per **6g** in 394 gallons. Low calcium causes foam and corrosion.

## Weekly Chemical Schedule

### Monday — Test & Balance Day
1. Test the water (strips or liquid kit) for bromine, pH, and TA.
2. Adjust **TA first** if outside 80–120 ppm.
3. Adjust **pH** to 7.4–7.6 after TA is set.
4. Check floater — refill bromine tablets as needed to maintain supply (~60–80g/week average).
5. Replenish your **scum ball** inspection (see below).

### Wednesday — Mid-Week Oxidizing Shock
1. After the last soak of the evening, dose **45g of MPS non-chlorine shock**.
2. Leave the cover off for 20–30 minutes with jets running.
3. This oxidizes organic waste (sweat, oils, lotions) and reactivates the bromide bank. This extends water life significantly.

### Saturday/Sunday — Heavy Use Shock
If you've had multiple users or a heavy use session, add a second **45g MPS shock** after the final soak. Always shock with the cover off and jets running.

---

## Monthly Tasks

**Filter Rinse** (every 1–2 weeks, more often with heavy use)
Remove the filter cartridge and rinse thoroughly with a garden hose, working top-to-bottom between the pleats. Do not use a high-pressure washer — it damages the fibers.

**Filter Chemical Soak** (monthly)
Soak the filter overnight in a filter cleaning solution or a diluted solution of **1 part muriatic acid to 20 parts water**. Rinse thoroughly before reinstalling. Always have a spare filter so the tub isn't out of service.

**Calcium Hardness Check** (monthly)
Soft water is damaging. If CH is below 150 ppm, dose calcium chloride accordingly.

**TDS Check** (monthly)
TDS climbs as chemicals accumulate over time. When TDS exceeds **1,500 ppm above your fill-water baseline**, it's time for a full drain.

## Scum Ball Cleaning Schedule

Scum balls (oil-absorbing sponges) trap body oils, lotions, and sunscreen that would otherwise cloud the water and clog your filter.

- **Daily/after each use:** Drop it back in after soaking. Give it a quick visual check.
- **Weekly:** Squeeze it out and rinse under warm water. You'll see it release oils.
- **Monthly:** Wash with a small amount of dish soap, rinse until the water runs clear, and let it dry fully before returning it.
- **Replace every 3–4 months** or when it no longer springs back to shape. A saturated scum ball does more harm than good.

---

## Filter Replacement Schedule

- **Rinse:** Every 1–2 weeks
- **Chemical soak:** Monthly
- **Full replacement:** Every **12 months** under normal use, or every **6–9 months** with heavy use. Even a clean-looking filter loses filtration efficiency over time as fibers break down.

---

## Full Water Change Schedule

For a 394-gallon hot tub under regular use (2–4 people, several times per week), drain and refill **every 3–4 months**. A simple formula:

**Drain interval (days) = Gallons ÷ (Daily bathers × 3)**

For your tub with ~2 average daily users: **394 ÷ (2 × 3) ≈ 65 days** (~2 months). With lighter use of 1 person a few times a week, you can comfortably stretch to **3–4 months**.

**At every drain:**
- Clean the shell with a non-foaming spa surface cleaner.
- Chemical-soak your filter.
- Wipe down the waterline with a spa scum remover.
- Re-establish your **sodium bromide bank (56g)** on the fresh fill before activating with MPS.

## Quick-Reference Weekly Card

| Day | Task |
|---|---|
| **Monday** | Test water → balance TA → balance pH → refill bromine floater |
| **Wednesday** | 45g MPS shock after last soak, cover off 30 min |
| **Fri/Sat** | Heavy use? Add 45g MPS shock after final soak |
| **Weekly** | Rinse scum ball, visually inspect filter |
| **Monthly** | Chemical soak filter, check calcium hardness & TDS |
| **Every 1–2 weeks** | Rinse filter cartridge |
| **Every 3–4 months** | Full drain, clean shell, refill, re-establish bromide bank |
| **Annually** | Replace filter cartridge |

> **Pro tip:** The biggest mistake hot tub owners make is skipping the mid-week MPS shock — it's what keeps the water from going cloudy and funky between full drain cycles.
